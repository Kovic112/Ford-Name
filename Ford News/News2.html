The Ford Mustang Outsold the Chevy Camaro Two to One Last Year

Welcome to The Grid, R&T's quick roundup of the auto industry and motorsports news you should know this morning.

If you've paid close attention to auto sales over the last few years, this isn't really surprising, but it is still stark. Automakers are releasing sales results for 2020, and Motor Authority found that Ford sold 61,090 Mustangs in 2020, while Chevrolet moved just 29,775 units of the Camaro. Both those figures are down around 13 percent when compared to 2019, undoubtedly a result of COVID-19. Dodge sold 52,995 Challengers last year, impressive given that it's a far older model than the Camaro and Mustang. That's what constant updates—and big V-8s—gets you.

The street race at St. Petersburg is the traditional IndyCar season opener, but COVID-19 forced its postponement to the end of the 2020 season, and it's already been postponed for the 2021 season. The IndyCar series announced yesterday that it's moving the St. Pete race to April 25, making the April 11 round at Barber Motorsports Park in Alabama the new season opener. "It's not surprising that an event of this magnitude, scheduled for the first week of March, is still subject to the implications of the pandemic," said Penske entertainment boss Mark Miles in a statement.

Racing Point Formula 1 has officially become Aston Martin for the 2021 season, and Autosport reports that it has a new title sponsor—American IT company Cognizant. BWT, the water treatment company that sponsored Racing Point, is leaving the team, and with that, the team's distinctive pink livery will likely become all green, reflecting Aston Martin's (and Britain's) traditional racing color.

Source: R&T - 07/01/2021
